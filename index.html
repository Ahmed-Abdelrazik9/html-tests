OSCE Scenario: Abdominal Pain Assessment

Patient Information

You are playing the role of Maria Chen, a 34-year-old woman who has come to the emergency department with severe abdominal pain that started approximately 8 hours ago.

Chief Complaint

"I have this terrible pain in my stomach. It started around my belly button but now it's moved to my right side and it's getting worse. I've never felt anything like this before."

History to Provide

  • Pain characteristics: Started as diffuse periumbilical pain 8 hours ago, now localized to right lower quadrant. Sharp, constant pain rated 8/10.
  • Associated symptoms: Nausea, vomited twice. Loss of appetite. No diarrhea or constipation.
  • Past medical history: Generally healthy. No previous surgeries.
  • Medications: Only takes occasional ibuprofen for menstrual cramps.
  • Allergies: None known.
  • Last menstrual period: 2 weeks ago, regular cycle.

Behavior Guidance

  • Appear visibly uncomfortable, occasionally wincing from pain.
  • Hold your right lower abdomen when moving or coughing.
  • Show anxiety about what might be causing the pain.
  • If the examiner presses on your right lower abdomen, react with increased pain (rebound tenderness).
  • Express concern about missing work tomorrow if asked about social history.

Key Information - Only If Asked

  • No recent travel history.
  • No history of inflammatory bowel disease.
  • No urinary symptoms.
  • No recent dietary changes or food poisoning symptoms.
  • Not sexually active in the past 3 months.
  • No history of similar pain episodes.

Expected Diagnosis

The candidate should suspect acute appendicitis based on the classic presentation of periumbilical pain migrating to the right lower quadrant, associated with nausea, vomiting, and anorexia.
