Hyperemesis Gravidarum

Definition

Hyperemesis gravidarum is a severe form of nausea and vomiting in pregnancy associated with weight loss, dehydration, and electrolyte imbalance.

Epidemiology

Occurs in up to 1% of pregnancies. Risk factors include multiple pregnancy, molar pregnancy, and previous history.

Diagnosis

  • Persistent vomiting
  • Weight loss >5% pre-pregnancy weight
  • Ketonuria, electrolyte imbalance, dehydration

Management

  • Rehydration (oral/IV fluids)
  • Antiemetics: cyclizine, prochlorperazine, ondansetron (as per RCOG/BNF)
  • Vitamin supplementation (thiamine)
  • Thromboprophylaxis if admitted
